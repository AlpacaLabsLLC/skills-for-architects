RE: 214 Maple Street, Brooklyn — Preliminary Zoning Review (EXCERPT, pp. 2-3 of 9)

The subject property is located within an M1-4/R6A Special Mixed Use District
(MX-8). The existing building is a four-story loft structure built c. 1926,
lot area approximately 10,000 sf, existing building footprint approximately
8,750 sf.

Under the R6A residential equivalent, the maximum residential FAR is 3.0.
For the contemplated commercial/office conversion, the applicable M1-4
commercial FAR is 2.0; community facility FAR is 4.8. The existing building
contains approximately 35,000 gsf, corresponding to a built FAR of
approximately 3.5 — the building is overbuilt relative to the commercial
FAR and any enlargement would requlre [sic] further analysis.

Maximum base height in R6A is 60 feet; maximum building height is 70 feet.
The existing parapet has been measured at 64'-6" (survey, Court Street Land
Surveying, 04/18).

MEZZANINE: Under ZR 12-10, a mezzanine occupying less than 33 1/3 percent
of the floor area of the story in which it is located is not counted as a
separate story but IS included in floor area calculations. Given the
overbuilt condition noted above, a new mezzanine would add floor area and
requires careful analysis before the client is advised it is feasible.
