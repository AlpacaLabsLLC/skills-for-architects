RE: 96 Greenpoint Avenue, Brooklyn — Preliminary Zoning & Use Review (EXCERPT, pp. 2-3 of 8)

The subject property is located within an R6A district with a C2-4
commercial overlay mapped along Greenpoint Avenue. The existing building
is a four-story mixed-use structure built 1931 on a corner lot; the
overlay extends 150 feet from the intersection and the full tenant space
falls within it.

An eating and drinking establishment is Use Group 6 and is permitted
as-of-right in C2-4. No size limitation applies to the contemplated
approximately 3,800 sf establishment; an establishment with entertainment
and a capacity of more than 200 persons would fall into Use Group 12,
which is not permitted in this overlay.

Under ZR 12-10, cellar space is excluded from floor area provided it
qualifies as a cellar — more than one-half its height below curb level.
The first floor slab has been measured at 2'-1" above curb at the corner
(Northside Measured Surveys, 05/28); on that basis the lower level appears
to qualify and its approximately 800 sf would not count toward floor area.
This shouId [sic] be confirmed against curb elevations on both frontages.

Occupant load: at 15 sf per person net for dining, the preliminary program
indicates a first-floor occupant load in excess of 74 persons. An eating
and drinking establishment above that threshold is classified Assembly
Group A-2, carrying sprinkler and two-exit requirements for the space.

CELLAR: Locating the prep kitchen and walk-in boxes in the cellar raises
three linked questions: whether the area remains excluded from floor area
once occupied as a kitchen (it does, if the cellar qualifies as above);
what occupant load the kitchen staff adds; and, most critically, egress.
A single interior stair serves the cellar, and the existing sidewalk hatch
is not a compliant second means of egress. Whether a second exit is
required turns on cellar occupant load and travel distance, and the
reported clearance of approximately 6'-10" below joists falls short of the
7'-6" required for occupiable space. This requires careful analysis before
the client is advised the cellar kitchen is feasible.
