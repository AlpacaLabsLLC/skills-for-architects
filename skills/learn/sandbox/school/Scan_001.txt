RE: 585 Lenox Road, Brooklyn — Preliminary Zoning Review (EXCERPT, pp. 3-4 of 11)

The subject property is a vacant through-lot extending from Lenox Road to
Linden Boulevard within an R6 district. Lot area is approximately 20,000 sf
(100 ft of frontage on each street; the Lenox Road frontage has been
measured at 100.00 ft per survey, Lenox Road Land Surveying, 04/28).

A school is a Use Group 3 community facility, permitted as-of-right in R6.
The applicable community facility FAR is 4.8, yielding a maximum of
approximately 96,000 zsf. The contemplated program of approximately
72,000 gsf for 450 students is comfortably within the permitted floor
area, and the controling [sic] constraint will be height and setback
rather than FAR.

Under the height factor regulations, the front wall may rise to a maximum
base height of 60 feet at the street line; above that, the building must
set back beneath a sky exposure plane rising at a slope of 2.7 to 1 over
Lenox Road, a narrow street. As a through-lot, the site has two front
walls and no rear yard, but a rear yard equivalent of 60 feet must be
provided at the midpoint of the lot unless distributed per the through-lot
provisions — this shapes where the upper floors can sit.

ROOFTOP PLAY YARD: An open play area on the roof is conceptually
consistent with the community facility use, but the required safety
enclosure is the open question. Guardrail-height fencing may qualify as a
permitted obstruction above the maximum base height; the taller fencing or
netting a school yard realistically requires (10 to 12 feet) may not, and
where the roof meets the sky exposure plane any enclosure could constitute
a penetration. Whether the enclosure is treated as an open fence, a
structural appurtenance, or an enlargement of the building is
determinative, and this requires careful analysis before the client is
advised the rooftop yard is feasible.
