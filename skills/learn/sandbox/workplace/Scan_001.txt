RE: Meridian Underwriting, 200 Water Street, Floors 12-13 — Preliminary Code
Review (EXCERPT, pp. 3-4 of 11)

The subject premises comprise the full 12th and 13th floors of a 31-story
office tower constructed c. 1971. Construction classification is Type I-B
(noncombustible, protected). The proposed occupancy is Group B (business).
The building is fully sprinklered thronghout [sic]; the tenant floors were
retrofitted under the base building program completed in 2018.

Floor plates are approximately 19,200 gsf each. At the business occupancy
factor of 1 occupant per 150 sf gross, the design occupant load is 128
occupants per floor, 256 occupants for the two-floor premises. Note the
client's stated headcount of 240 does not govern; the calculated load does.

Each floor is served by two existing exit stairs. Nominal clear width is
44 inches; Stair B was field-measured on the 12th floor landing at 43-1/2
inches between handrails (site verification, 06/12). At the sprinklered
egress capacity factor of 0.2 inches per occupant, the measured width yields
a capacity of 217 occupants per stair, 434 per floor — well in excess of the
128-occupant per-floor load. The existing stairs are adequate for the
proposed occupancy without modification.

INTERCONNECTING STAIR: The proposed new stair connecting the 12th and 13th
floors requires a new opening in the 13th floor slab. Under the vertical
opening provisions of the Building Code, a floor opening connecting not more
than two stories within a single tenancy may be permitted without a shaft
enclosure, subject to specific conditions on the extent of the opening and
its separation from the exit access serving other portions of the floors.
Whether the town hall configuration the client has described satisfies those
conditions is not yet established. Separately, the landlord's alteration
agreement requires structural review and consent for any slab penetration in
this 1971 building. This item requires careful analysis before the client is
advised that the stair is feasible.
