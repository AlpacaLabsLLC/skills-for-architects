RE: 47-11 Parsons Boulevard, Queens — Preliminary Zoning Review (EXCERPT, pp. 2-3 of 8)

The subject property is located within an R5 district with a C1-2 commercial
overlay. The existing building is a three-story medical office structure
built 1958, lot area approximately 21,200 sf, existing building footprint
approximately 9,800 sf.

An outpatient ambulatory diagnostic and treatment facility is a Use Group 4
community facility use, permitted as-of-right in R5. The applicable
community facility FAR is 2.0. The existing building contains approximately
28,400 gsf, corresponding to a built FAR of approximately 1.34. The proposed
infusion suite addition of approximately 6,400 gsf would bring the total to
approximately 34,800 gsf, or an FAR of approximately 1.64 — within the
permitted community facility floor area, subject to confirmation of the
deeded lot area.

In R5, the maximum height of a front wall is 30 feet, above which a sky
exposure plane applies. The existing parapet has been measured at 41'-9"
(survey, Parsons Blvd Land Surveying, 04/28), making the existing building
non-complying as to height.

ENLARGEMENT / MRI SUITE: The proposed addition appears to fit within the
permitted floor area noted above. However, under ZR 54-31, an enlargement
of a non-complying building may not create a new non-compliance or increase
the degree of an existing non-compliance; the addition must therefore be
held entirely below the sky exposure plane, and its placement on the lot is
constrained accordingly. Relocation of imaging uses to the second floor
within the existlng [sic] envelope raises no floor area question, but the
combined effect of the addition and the height non-compliance requires
careful analysis before the client is advised it is feasible.
