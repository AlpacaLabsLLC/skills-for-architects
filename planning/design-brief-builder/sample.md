# Design Brief — The Foundry Cowork

## 1. Executive Summary

A 12,000 SF adaptive reuse of a brick-and-timber warehouse in Portland, OR into a 150-member coworking space serving freelancers and small teams, with a strong emphasis on community programming and events. The core design driver is creating a space that feels more like a neighborhood than an office — where chance encounters, shared meals, and evening events are as important as the desks and meeting rooms.

## 4. Design Criteria

- **Aesthetic direction** — Industrial-warm. Retain and celebrate the existing heavy timber structure, exposed brick, and steel connections. Layer in warmth through wood furniture, textile panels, and living plants. Avoid the sterile "tech office" look — this should feel like a living room that happens to have great Wi-Fi. References: Industrious Portland, NeueHouse (material warmth), Fosbury & Sons Antwerp (residential feel in a commercial space).

- **Material preferences** — Polished concrete floors (existing slab) with area rugs in lounge zones. White-oak plywood for custom millwork and desk dividers. Acoustic felt panels in charcoal and terracotta. Black steel for shelving and signage frames. Leather and bouclé upholstery for lounge seating. Budget-conscious: splurge on the café counter and reception desk as signature moments.

- **Sustainability targets** — Pursue Fitwel 2-Star certification (community health focus over pure energy). Salvaged and reclaimed materials where possible (doors, hardware, furniture). Low-VOC finishes throughout. Target 50% reduction in embodied carbon versus conventional tenant improvement. Drought-tolerant plantings on terrace.

- **Daylighting and views** — The warehouse has tall windows on two sides (north and west). Prioritize open workspace and event space along these exposures. Team offices should be interior but with glazed partitions to borrow daylight. No regularly occupied space should be more than 30 feet from a window.

- **Brand / identity** — Trellis positions itself as "the coworking space for people who don't like coworking spaces." The design should feel curated and personal, not corporate. Local art program: rotating exhibitions from Portland artists. Signage and wayfinding should be warm, hand-crafted in feel — not slick. Color palette: warm neutrals with terracotta and forest green accents.

## 5. Technical Requirements

- **Structural** — Existing heavy timber post-and-beam structure with 20-ft clear spans. Timber columns on a 20'x20' grid. Roof structure is exposed bowstring trusses — confirm load capacity for any suspended elements (lighting rigs, acoustic clouds). Ground floor slab: verify condition and levelness before specifying polished concrete finish.

- **MEP**
  - *HVAC:* Exposed ductwork (spiral galvanized). VRF system recommended for zone control — team offices, event space, and open workspace need independent thermostat zones. Café kitchen requires dedicated exhaust (Type I commercial hood).
  - *Electrical:* 400A service (confirm with utility). Every desk position needs two outlets and one USB-C port. Floor boxes in open workspace areas (coordinate with slab condition). Event space needs 60A dedicated circuit for AV and catering.
  - *Plumbing:* Existing 4" sanitary main. Add two restroom cores, café kitchen rough-in, and mother's room sink. Verify grease trap requirement for commercial kitchen use.

- **Code**
  - *Occupancy:* B (business) for workspace zones, A-2 (assembly) for event space. Mixed-use occupancy separation required at event space boundary.
  - *Egress:* Two exits required (confirm existing door locations). Event space at 80 standing occupancy requires minimum 44" exit width. Verify travel distances.
  - *Fire protection:* Existing building likely non-sprinklered. Change of occupancy to A-2 may trigger sprinkler requirement — confirm with building official early.
  - *Accessibility:* ADA-compliant throughout. Accessible route from street to all public areas. If mezzanine is added, elevator or lift required.

- **Technology**
  - *Network:* Minimum 1 Gbps symmetrical fiber, redundant ISP. Managed Wi-Fi (enterprise-grade APs, target 1 AP per 1,000 SF). Wired ethernet to every team office and meeting room.
  - *AV:* Meeting rooms: 55" display + USB-C dock + ceiling mic/speaker. Workshop: ceiling-mounted projector, wireless presentation, 2-mic PA. Event space: portable PA, projector screen, theatrical lighting rig on dimmer.
  - *Access control:* Keycard or mobile-app entry at front door, team office doors, and after-hours zones. Integration with coworking management platform (Optix, Nexudus, or similar).
  - *Security:* Camera coverage at entry, reception, and loading dock. No cameras in workspace areas.

## 7. Success Criteria

- At least 75% of regularly occupied desk positions receive direct or borrowed natural daylight.
- Event space converts from daytime workshop layout to evening event configuration in under 20 minutes with two staff.
- Ambient noise in the dedicated desk area does not exceed 45 dBA during normal business hours.
- Focus booths achieve minimum STC 35 rating.
- A new member can sign up, receive access credentials, and find an available hot desk within 5 minutes of arriving.
- The café kitchen supports catered events for 80 people without disrupting daytime workspace operations.
- 90% of members report the space "feels like a community, not just a workplace" in post-occupancy survey.
- Building achieves Fitwel 2-Star certification within 6 months of occupancy.
- Terrace is usable at least 8 months of the year (rain cover, heating for shoulder seasons).

## 8. Open Questions

### Must Resolve Before SD

- **Fire protection:** Does the change of occupancy to A-2 (assembly) trigger a full sprinkler requirement? Meet with building official before SD to confirm. This could add $120K+ to the budget.
- **Slab condition:** Is the existing concrete slab level and structurally adequate for polished-concrete finish, or does it need an overlay? Core samples needed.
- **Lease terms:** Does the landlord contribute to tenant improvements (TI allowance)? This affects budget allocation significantly.
- **Kitchen classification:** Is the café operator a separate tenant or member amenity? This changes code requirements (commercial kitchen licensing, grease trap, Type I hood sizing).

### Resolve During SD

- **Mezzanine:** The 20-ft ceiling height could support a mezzanine over the team office zone, adding ~1,200 SF. Is this desirable? It would require an elevator/lift for accessibility and affects the open, lofty feel.
- **Acoustic strategy:** Open plan coworking vs. warehouse acoustics is a known challenge. Explore ceiling cloud options, acoustic felt wraps on columns, and sound masking systems. Budget implications TBD.
- **After-hours access model:** Do all members get 24/7 access, or only dedicated-desk and team-office members? This affects security zoning and access control scope.
- **Outdoor terrace weather protection:** Full roof cover vs. partial pergola vs. retractable awning? Each has different cost and permit implications.

### Can Defer to DD

- **Furniture selection:** Specific desk, chair, and lounge furniture models. Develop a furniture budget and test in mock-up during DD.
- **Art program:** Rotating gallery details — artist selection process, hanging system, lighting, insurance.
- **Signage and wayfinding:** Detailed design of room signs, directories, and brand touchpoints.
- **Planting plan:** Species selection for terrace planters and interior green walls (if desired).
- **AV vendor:** Final selection of meeting room and event AV packages. Get competitive bids during DD.

## 9. Appendix

### Precedent Projects

- **Industrious Portland** — Material palette reference (warm wood, exposed structure, residential furnishings in a commercial context).
- **NeueHouse Hollywood** — Community programming model and event space flexibility. How a coworking brand builds culture through curated events.
- **Fosbury & Sons, Antwerp** — Adaptive reuse of a historic building into coworking. Strong residential aesthetic — rugs, art, soft lighting — that makes the space feel lived-in rather than corporate.
- **Second Home Lisbon** — Biophilic design in a coworking context. Abundant planting integrated into the architecture, not just decorative pots.
- **Wayfare Costa Mesa** — Community-first coworking model. Shared kitchen as social anchor. Events drive membership more than desk space.

### Relevant Codes and Standards

- **IBC 2021** (Oregon adopted edition) — Mixed occupancy B/A-2, sprinkler requirements, egress calculations.
- **Oregon Structural Specialty Code** — Existing building provisions, unreinforced masonry (if applicable), seismic upgrade requirements for change of occupancy.
