## 5. Neighborhood Context

### Neighborhood Character

Hudson Square occupies a transitional zone between Tribeca to the south, the West Village to the north, SoHo to the east, and the Hudson River waterfront to the west. The built environment is characterized by mid-rise industrial and commercial loft buildings from the early-to-mid 20th century (6–12 stories), many of which have been converted to Class A office space. Since the 2013 rezoning, several residential towers of 25–35 stories have been completed or approved, creating a rapidly evolving skyline.

The streetscape is generally wide and well-maintained, with newer streetscaping along Hudson and Greenwich Streets. Ground-floor retail is increasing but still less dense than neighboring SoHo — the area has more of a commercial-professional character than a retail-entertainment one.

## 6. Natural Features & Hazards

### Topography

- **Elevation:** Approximately 10–15 ft (3–5 m) above sea level (NAVD88)
- **Slope:** Essentially flat — grade change across the site is negligible
- **Historic note:** Much of the area west of Greenwich Street is built on landfill added in the 18th and 19th centuries. The original Manhattan shoreline ran roughly along Greenwich Street.

### Flood Zones (FEMA)

| Zone | Designation | Applicability |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| Zone X | Minimal flood hazard | Site appears to be in Zone X per current FEMA FIRMs |
| Zone AE | 1% annual-chance flood | Begins approximately 2 blocks west (west of Washington Street) |
| Preliminary FEMA maps (2015) | Expanded Zone AE | ⚠️ **Needs field verification** — Preliminary updated flood maps show expanded flood zones that may affect the western portion of this block. Final adoption pending. |

- NYC Climate Resiliency Design Guidelines recommend designing ground floors to DFE + 2 ft freeboard for sites near the flood zone boundary.

### Seismic Risk

- **ASCE 7-22 Seismic Design Category:** B
- **Site Class:** D (stiff soil) assumed; geotechnical investigation required for confirmation
- **General risk:** Low to moderate. New York City is not on a major fault boundary, but the Ramapo Fault system is ~25 mi northwest. The NYC Building Code requires seismic design per ASCE 7.

### Soil & Geotechnical Conditions

- **General conditions:** Fill over native glacial deposits (sand, gravel, clay). Depth to bedrock (Manhattan schist) in this area is estimated at 60–100 ft below grade.
- **Groundwater:** Likely encountered at 8–15 ft below grade due to proximity to the Hudson River and low elevation.
- **Foundation considerations:** Deep foundations (driven piles or drilled shafts to bedrock) typical for buildings above 10 stories in this area. A full geotechnical investigation is required.

### Vegetation

- Street trees present along Hudson Street (London Plane trees typical in this area)
- No significant natural vegetation — fully urbanized site
- Any street tree removal requires NYC Parks Department approval and replacement

### Water Bodies

- **Hudson River:** 0.3 mi (500 m) west of the site
- No wetlands or surface water features on or immediately adjacent to the site

### Environmental Contamination

- Hudson Square's industrial history (printing, light manufacturing) means brownfield conditions are common in the area. A Phase I Environmental Site Assessment (ESA) is strongly recommended.
- No active Superfund sites within 0.25 mi per EPA CERCLIS database (search as of 2025)
- NYC E-Designation or (E) restrictive declaration may apply — check NYC OER records for this specific lot

**Sources:** FEMA Flood Map Service Center (FIRM panels 360497); USGS National Elevation Dataset; NYC DEP; EPA Envirofacts; ASCE 7-22; NYC Climate Resiliency Design Guidelines (2022)

## Gaps & Caveats

- **Lot-specific zoning confirmation required.** This analysis is based on the general zoning for the area (M1-6 / Hudson Square Special District). A formal zoning determination from NYC DCP or a zoning attorney should confirm the exact applicable FAR, height limits, and sub-area designation for the specific tax lot.
- ⚠️ **FEMA flood maps are in flux.** The preliminary 2015 updated FEMA maps for NYC have not been finalized. The site's flood zone status could change with final map adoption. Confirm current and projected flood risk before design.
- ⚠️ **Adjacent parcels need field verification.** Construction activity in Hudson Square is ongoing. Conditions on adjacent lots may have changed since the most recent aerial imagery and public records.
- **Geotechnical investigation required.** Soil conditions, depth to bedrock, groundwater levels, and potential contamination require a professional geotechnical investigation and Phase I ESA. Desk research cannot substitute for subsurface exploration.
- **Title search and survey required.** Easements, deed restrictions, party wall agreements, and encroachments can only be confirmed through a professional title search and ALTA survey.
- **Landmark adjacency.** Proximity to the Charlton-King-Vandam Historic District may trigger LPC review for new construction visible from the district. Confirm with LPC whether the site falls within a "buffer" or advisory review area.
- **Demographic and market data.** Census data reflects 2020 estimates. Housing market data is approximate and changes rapidly. Engage a market analyst for investment-grade projections.
- **Wind study.** Street-level wind conditions in this corridor may require a pedestrian wind comfort study for towers above 200 ft, per NYC DCP guidelines.
